# ASM International N.V. — Company Intelligence Brief
*March 2026 | Ticker: ASMIY (ADR) | Amsterdam: ASM*

---

## 1. Company Snapshot

ASM International (Almere, NL) is the world's dominant pure-play supplier of Atomic Layer Deposition (ALD) equipment — the deposition technology that is structurally non-negotiable at every advanced node below 10nm. With €3.17B in FY2025 revenue, >55% share of the $3B single-wafer ALD market, and 9 consecutive years of double-digit growth, ASM sits at the critical-path intersection of AI infrastructure buildout and semiconductor scaling.

---

## 2. The Moat

**Technology leadership — 20-year head start.** ASM pioneered ALD for high-volume manufacturing at Intel's 45nm node in 2007. ALD steps per chip scale from ~20 at 28nm to 80+ at 2nm, and grow further at 1.4nm — meaning every node transition structurally expands ASM's SAM. Current-generation innovations (Mo ALD, Area Selective Deposition) are already in HVM at 2nm.

**IP fortress.** 2,953+ patents, €498M gross R&D (15.7% of revenue), and a 35-year IMEC partnership create a compounding IP moat that is practically impossible to replicate on a competitive timeline.

**Market position.** >55% share in a niche that competitors (AMAT, Lam, TEL) cannot easily displace — they are 4–8× larger by total revenue but lack ASM's single-wafer ALD depth. Best-in-class gross margin (51.8% vs. AMAT 47–49%, Lam 46–47%, TEL ~45%) reflects genuine pricing power, not mix luck.

## 4. Growth Catalysts

- **Node transitions drive structural SAM expansion.** 1.4nm pilot line investments expected H2 2026 — each new node adds $450–500M of incremental SAM per 100k wafers. GAA at 2nm already added ~$400M SAM. This is compounding, not cyclical.
- **China re-acceleration.** China >30% of revenue; management upgraded the outlook from decline to growth in 2026. Any easing of export control pressure is asymmetric upside.
- **ALD market growth to $5.1–6.1B by 2030.** CAGR of 9–13%; ASM's own 2030 target is >€5.7B revenue — sustained ~10% annual growth at maintained or expanded share.
- **Axus acquisition + new CPO.** CMP capability expands process step coverage; unified tech/product leadership under Dr. Gary Ding accelerates roadmap into 1.4nm and beyond.

---

## 5. Key Risks

- **China export control escalation.** >30% revenue exposure; any incremental US/Dutch restrictions on advanced ALD tooling creates material near-term headwinds.
- **Bookings transparency reduction.** Discontinuing quarterly bookings disclosure from Q1 2026 — removes the key leading indicator for investors.
- **Applied Materials competitive pressure.** Post-Picosun, AMAT is the only credible PEALD challenger with scale and R&D budget to contest ASM's niche over time.

---

## 6. Verdict

ASM International is a structurally advantaged compounder in a non-cyclical role: every chip generation at the leading edge requires more ALD, and ASM owns that market. At ~$40.7B market cap with record margins, FCF of €615M, and a Q1 2026 guide implying 19% YoY growth, the business is executing at peak. The primary re-rating risk is geopolitical (China controls), not competitive — and the 2030 roadmap to >€5.7B revenue is credible given the technology pipeline. For investors with a 3–5 year horizon, ASM remains a high-conviction way to own AI-driven semiconductor capex without direct chip cycle exposure.
